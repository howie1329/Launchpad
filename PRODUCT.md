# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Launchpad is for solo developers, indie hackers, and technical founders who use AI heavily while deciding what is worth building. They are usually working alone or in a very small team, moving between rough ideas, product questions, PRD drafts, project context, and implementation planning.

They need more structure than a blank chat thread, but less ceremony than a project management suite. Their main job is to turn messy thinking into scoped, buildable work without losing the conversation, decisions, or context that got them there.

## Product Purpose

Launchpad is a chat-first workspace where product thinking becomes durable. Users start in conversation, preserve useful decisions as markdown artifacts, organize mature work into projects, import external AI context, and continue with an assistant that can read and write workspace state.

Success means a builder can move from a rough thought to a named project, useful artifacts, clear scope, and bounded AI usage without feeling pushed into heavyweight process.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, capable, pragmatic.

Launchpad should feel like a thinking workspace for serious builders: calm enough for uncertain early ideas, structured enough to produce real artifacts, and direct enough to avoid performative planning. The product voice is concise, specific, and grounded in what the user can do next.

## Anti-references

- Heavyweight PM suites that make planning feel like the work.
- Generic AI chat wrappers that lose context between sessions.
- Decorative SaaS marketing, oversized cards, gradient-heavy surfaces, and theatrical productivity language.
- Dense enterprise admin chrome that makes a solo builder feel like they are operating a system built for a committee.
- Magic-feeling AI flows that overwrite user intent instead of proposing drafts for review.

## Design Principles

- **Keep thinking close to doing.** Conversations, artifacts, projects, and actions should feel like one continuous workspace.
- **Make durable state visible.** Artifacts, project context, memory, imports, usage, and history should be legible without stealing focus from the current task.
- **Prefer explicit review over magic.** AI-generated drafts, promotions, imports, and mutations should be inspectable before they become durable workspace state.
- **Stay dense but breathable.** The UI should support repeated work and quick scanning without becoming cold, cramped, or enterprise-heavy.
- **Reduce ceremony.** Every screen should help the user make the next product decision, not perform process for its own sake.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for core product flows. Preserve keyboard access across navigation, command palette, dialogs, menus, editor surfaces, and chat input. Maintain visible focus states, contrast-safe foreground and muted text colors, clear loading and error states, and reduced-motion alternatives for all transitions.
